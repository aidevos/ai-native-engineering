# 01 — Own the judgment, delegate the execution

AI is genuinely good at execution: generating options, writing boilerplate, finding
patterns, synthesizing documentation. It is not good at judgment: deciding what matters,
what the tradeoffs are, what the user actually needs.

The engineer who misidentifies which one they're responsible for is setting themselves up
to be the first to become optional.

You are paid for taste, problem framing, and decisions made under uncertainty. Those are
not commodities that scale to zero. The lines of code you personally typed are.
